Book Review

8/10

-1984 By George Orwell-

  • This was the first Dystopian Future novel I had ever read, and opened my eyes to a whole new world of content
  • I was especially impressed with the world that Orwell had created, and the detail put into the government's structure
  • I usually read a chapter a night, but by the last few chapters I was stuck to the novel and finished it all in one sitting

8/10

-The Master and Margarita by Mikhail Bulgakov-

  • This novel, originally written in Russian, was the first translated media I had ever read; Although, a difficult task at times
  • Many chapters were quite dense, and I often struggled to understand the plot due to the language
  • I obtained some outside sources in order to help me grasp the text, as I felt I was missing key details
  • Overall, the payoff was excellent, as the ending of this story felt magical, and I cannot wait to read it again

9/10

-Kafka on The Shore by Haruki Murakami-

  • This novel, similar to The M&M, felt extremely magical at times
  • I enjoyed this story, as it focused on the main characters inner thoughts, even personifying them into another character named "crow"
  • Originally written in Japanese, there were many Western references within the story, and it was interesting to read from my westernized perspective

8/10

-I Am Legend by Richard Matheson-

  • Usually, I am not a fan of post-apocalyptic stories, however, this book entails so much more than that
  • I enjoyed this story as it allowed me to reflect on who the "bad guys" really are; I found myself at times sympathizing with the vampires
  • As well, the ending was one of the best I had ever read
  • The process of understanding why the novel is called I Am Legend is a journey I will never forget

8.5/10

-The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho-

  • The best way to describe this novel is with one word: Peaceful
  • This novel takes you on a journey with only you and the main character's thoughts
  • I found that the chapters I enjoyed the most were the ones filled with the main characters rambling thoughts, which can sound boring but in all actuality was a great adventure

7/10

-The Kite Runner by Khaled Hosseini-

  • This was an excellent read, however at times a bit too sad for my liking
  • I enjoyed the subtle connections that the book would make with itself, most notably the lines spoken at the end of the final chapter
  • This novel also opened my eyes to many of the troubles countries are facing across the world, some of which we don't even hear about in North America

8/10

-Brave New World by Aldous Huxley-

  • My favourite aspect of this novel is the writing style of the author. The story was fantastic, however, it was really the author's way of phrasing sentences that drew me in
  • Similar to 1984, this novel amazed me in the world that was created, and the mechanisms used by the government to control its citizens
  • However, I was not extremely impressed with the ending. The beginning of this book is definitely my favourite aspect

8.5/10

-The Stranger by Albert Camus-

  • This was the first philosophy book I had ever read; I chose it because it was short and thought it would be a good start
  • This book is similar to The Alchemist, where my favourite chapters consisted of the main character living his ordinary life and hearing his inner thoughts
  • I found myself bookmarking numerous pages within this novel, as there are so many gems within quotes
